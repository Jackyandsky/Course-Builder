The Canon: Close Reading Part III - The High Renaissance

Course Overview

This third course in "The Canon: Close Reading" series immerses students in the vibrant literary landscape of the High Renaissance. Focusing on influential works that embody the period's intellectual and artistic fervor, students will analyze texts that explore humanism, political philosophy, courtly ideals, epic poetry, and burgeoning dramatic forms. The curriculum emphasizes advanced close reading techniques, understanding the interplay between classical revival and contemporary innovation, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Renaissance Humanism and Thought

Close reading of texts to understand core Renaissance ideas: the dignity of man, secular political theory, ideals of courtly behavior, the exploration of human nature, and the revival of classical forms and philosophies.

Interpreting Diverse Renaissance Genres

Developing skills to analyze a range of genres including political treatises, courtesy books, satirical prose, frame tales, epic poetry, and early modern drama, noting their distinct conventions and purposes.

Advanced Analytical Essays (5/5/5 Method)

Crafting well-structured and insightful analytical essays that explore thematic complexities, rhetorical strategies, and the cultural significance of High Renaissance literature. Continued emphasis on LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected authors/works (or substantial excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar discussions on literary techniques, historical context (e.g., Italian city-states, French Renaissance, Elizabethan England), and philosophical underpinnings. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, honing their ability to articulate nuanced interpretations with textual evidence and critical insight. The course aims to provide a deep appreciation for the literary achievements of the High Renaissance and their lasting impact.

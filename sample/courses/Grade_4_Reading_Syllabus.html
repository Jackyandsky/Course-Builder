Grade 4 Reading

Course Overview

This Grade 4 Reading course is designed to enhance students' ability to comprehend and analyze increasingly complex narrative and informational texts. Through a selection of engaging chapter books, students will develop critical thinking skills, expand their vocabulary, and learn to identify and discuss various literary elements. The curriculum focuses on fostering independent reading habits and a deeper appreciation for diverse genres and authorial styles, preparing students for more advanced literary study.

Educational Approach

Advanced Comprehension Strategies

Focus on applying strategies such as making connections (text-to-text, text-to-self, text-to-world), questioning the text, determining importance of information, and synthesizing ideas from different parts of a story.

Analysis of Literary Elements

Guiding students to analyze character development and motivations, complex plot structures (including subplots), identifying themes and author's message, and understanding figurative language (similes, metaphors, personification).

Vocabulary Expansion and Contextual Understanding

Expanding academic and literary vocabulary through direct instruction and contextual analysis within the readings. Encouraging students to use new vocabulary in discussions and responses.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 4 Reading course is structured around the in-depth exploration of ten diverse chapter books, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each novel. Each unit includes guided reading activities, interactive discussions that promote critical thinking, and exercises designed to enhance specific comprehension skills. Students will learn to articulate their understanding of the texts through oral responses and written reflections. The course aims to build reading stamina, analytical capabilities, and a strong foundation for appreciating nuanced literature.

Curated 1:4 Grade 4 Reading & Writing

Course Overview

This curated Grade 4 Reading & Writing course is designed to foster a love of literature while developing essential comprehension, analytical thinking, and foundational writing skills. Students will journey through a variety of engaging novels and short stories, learning to identify key literary elements such as perspective, allegory, and characterization. They will practice articulating their understanding through structured writing, including the 3/5/5 essay format, and build a strong vocabulary base with weekly exercises. This small-group (1:4) setting allows for personalized attention and interactive learning.

Educational Approach

Foundational Writing Skills (3/5/5 Essay)

Introduction to structured essay writing using the 3/5/5 method (3 paragraphs, 5 sentences per paragraph, 5 vocabulary words), focusing on clear expression, paragraph construction, and vocabulary application tailored for Grade 4 students.

Reading Comprehension & Literary Analysis

Development of reading comprehension through guided questions, interactive discussions, and activities. Students learn to identify and analyze plot, character, setting, theme, perspective, and allegory in age-appropriate texts.

Vocabulary Building and Craft Discussions

Weekly vocabulary quizzes (Letters A-Z, adapted for Grade 4) and engaging craft discussions on topics like finding textual evidence, creative writing basics, understanding characters, and identifying literary devices.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-week program guides Grade 4 students through ten diverse literary works in a 1:4 teacher-student ratio. Each unit, typically spanning three weeks, includes weekly vocabulary exercises, reading reviews, and practice with the 3/5/5 essay format. Craft discussions focus on specific literary concepts relevant to the texts, such as understanding perspective in "Tuck Everlasting," identifying allegory in "Watership Down," or analyzing characterization in "Wonder." The course includes a midterm quiz and essay, as well as a final review quiz and creative writing/essay assignment, with ample support in the small-group setting.

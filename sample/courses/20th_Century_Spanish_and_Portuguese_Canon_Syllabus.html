Advanced Reading: 20th Century Spanish & Portuguese Canon

Course Overview

This advanced reading course provides an intensive exploration of six pivotal works from the 20th-century Spanish and Portuguese literary canons, primarily focusing on Latin American authors. Students will engage with masterpieces by authors such as Isabel Allende, Gabriel García Márquez, Jorge Luis Borges, Carlos Fuentes, Mario Vargas Llosa, and José Saramago. The curriculum emphasizes understanding themes of magical realism, historical sagas, political turmoil, philosophical inquiry, and societal critique, while honing analytical writing skills through the 5/5/5 method and enriching vocabulary with the LEX program.

Educational Approach

Magical Realism and Postmodern Narratives

Analysis of key literary movements and styles prevalent in 20th-century Spanish and Portuguese literature, particularly magical realism (Márquez, Allende) and postmodern narrative techniques (Borges, Saramago), exploring their impact on storytelling and thematic development.

5/5/5 Analytical Essay Writing

Students will develop sophisticated essay writing skills using the 5/5/5 structure to analyze complex characters, historical allegories, political critiques (Fuentes, Vargas Llosa), and philosophical explorations of reality, memory, and truth.

LEX Vocabulary & Socio-Historical Context

Comprehensive vocabulary acquisition via the LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z), integrated with discussions on the socio-political history of Latin America and the Iberian Peninsula, including themes of revolution, dictatorship, and cultural identity, to provide a robust framework for literary interpretation.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around five-week intensives for each of the six selected canonical works. Each unit involves focused reading (approximately 100-125 pages per week), regular LEX vocabulary quizzes (covering letters A-Z cumulatively throughout the course), and in-depth class discussions based on "Questions to Ponder" specific to each text. Students will complete multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays for each work, exploring themes such as family sagas, political corruption, the nature of power, and societal responses to crisis. The course incorporates critical essays on Latin American and Luso-Hispanic literature, as well as primers on relevant historical events and literary movements.

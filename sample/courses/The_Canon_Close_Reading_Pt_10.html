The Canon: Close Reading Part X - Modernist & Postmodernist Voices

Course Overview

This tenth installment of "The Canon: Close Reading" series focuses on pivotal works of Modernist and Postmodernist literature from the 20th century. Students will engage in intensive textual analysis of influential authors who challenged traditional narrative forms and explored themes of alienation, consciousness, existentialism, and societal fragmentation. The course aims to further hone advanced interpretive skills, introduce relevant critical theories, and cultivate sophisticated analytical writing through the 5/5/5 essay method and LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Advanced Close Reading of Complex Narratives

Mastering techniques for analyzing stream of consciousness, non-linear plots, unreliable narrators, symbolism, and intertextuality prevalent in Modernist and Postmodernist literature.

Engagement with Literary and Critical Theory

Introduction to critical lenses relevant to 20th-century literature, such as formalism, psychoanalysis, existentialism, structuralism, and post-structuralism, to deepen interpretive possibilities.

Sophisticated Analytical Essays (5/5/5 Method)

Crafting nuanced and well-supported analytical essays that engage with the complex themes, philosophical underpinnings, and innovative techniques of the selected texts. Continued emphasis on LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected seminal works or authors. Each unit involves rigorous close reading of assigned texts, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar-style discussions exploring complex literary questions and critical perspectives. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, focusing on advanced literary analysis and critical argumentation. The course aims to equip students with the skills to confidently navigate and interpret challenging canonical literature from the 20th century.

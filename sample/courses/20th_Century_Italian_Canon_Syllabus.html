20th Century Italian Canon

Course Overview

This course explores key works from the 20th-century Italian literary canon, featuring authors such as Umberto Eco, Italo Svevo, Cesare Pavese, Italo Calvino, and Luigi Pirandello. Students will analyze themes of history and heresy, consciousness and addiction, post-war societal shifts, postmodern narrative experimentation, and the nature of identity. The curriculum is designed to cultivate advanced critical reading, sophisticated analytical writing through the 5/5/5 essay method, and a comprehensive vocabulary via the LEX program.

Educational Approach

Exploration of Italian Literary Movements

Analysis of distinct Italian literary contributions, including Eco's semiotic mysteries, Svevo's psychoanalytic narratives, Pavese's neorealism, Calvino's postmodern experimentation, and Pirandello's explorations of identity and perception.

5/5/5 Analytical Essay Framework

Students will refine their essay writing by employing the 5/5/5 structure to analyze character development, thematic complexities (e.g., symbols, narrative voice, frame narratives, duality), and the authors' unique stylistic choices within the Italian literary context.

LEX Vocabulary & Cultural Context

Systematic development of advanced vocabulary using the complete LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z), integrated with discussions of Italian history, Catholicism, the World Wars' impact, and the rise of modern Italian society to provide a rich backdrop for literary interpretation.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected Italian canonical works. Weekly activities involve reading approximately 100-125 pages, regular LEX vocabulary quizzes (cumulatively covering letters A-Z), and in-depth class discussions addressing "Questions to Ponder" for each text. Students will complete multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays focusing on character, theme, and literary concepts such as symbolism and narrative structure. The course includes engagement with critical essays on Italian literature, analyses of Italian neorealism in film, and the legacy of modernity in Italian storytelling.

Curated 1:4 Grade 6 Reading & Writing

Course Overview

This curated Grade 6 Reading & Writing course is tailored for students seeking to advance their literacy skills in a focused 1:4 teacher-student environment. The curriculum explores a range of compelling novels, short stories, and an introduction to Shakespeare, aiming to deepen critical thinking, analytical abilities, and sophisticated writing techniques. Students will engage with themes of societal structures, individual freedom, morality, and human nature, while mastering the 5/5/5 essay format and expanding their vocabulary through the LEX Intermediate program.

Educational Approach

Analytical Essay Writing (5/5/5 Method)

Mastery of the 5/5/5 essay structure, focusing on developing strong thesis statements, providing robust textual evidence, analyzing literary devices (e.g., symbolism, allegory, irony), and crafting coherent arguments.

Critical Interpretation and Thematic Discussion

Engaging with complex themes such as dystopia, social justice, science vs. faith, and the nature of good and evil. "Questions to Ponder" facilitate in-depth discussions and critical interpretation of texts.

LEX Intermediate Vocabulary & Literary Concepts

Systematic vocabulary development (LEX Intermediate A-Z) and introduction to key literary concepts necessary for analyzing novels, short stories, and drama. Weekly quizzes reinforce learning.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course dedicates approximately 3-4 weeks to each of the eight selected literary works, ensuring thorough exploration in a supportive 1:4 setting. Weekly activities include LEX Intermediate vocabulary quizzes, detailed discussions on reading assignments, and focused instruction on essay writing techniques. Students will complete multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays, receiving personalized feedback to refine their skills. The course aims to equip Grade 6 students with strong analytical and writing foundations for future academic success.

Level 10-12 Advanced Reading: Bildungsromans

Course Overview

This advanced course for levels 10-12 offers an in-depth exploration of the Bildungsroman, or coming-of-age novel. Students will analyze how characters evolve in conjunction with their narratives, examining themes of individual growth, societal influence, identity formation, and the transition from youth to adulthood. The course covers a diverse selection of classic and contemporary Bildungsromans, emphasizing critical analysis of character development, narrative structure, and thematic purpose, alongside rigorous vocabulary building and essay writing.

Educational Approach

Genre Study: Bildungsroman & Künstlerroman

In-depth understanding of the tropes, conventions, themes (e.g., individual vs. society, identity, love, loss, exile), and evolution of the coming-of-age novel, including its artistic variant, the Künstlerroman.

Character Evolution Analysis

Focus on tracking and analyzing character growth, regression, or stagnation. Discussions explore formative experiences, moral development, and the impact of societal, cultural, and personal challenges on protagonists.

Advanced Essay Writing & LEX Vocabulary

Rigorous practice in analytical writing using the 5/5/5 essay structure, alongside comprehensive vocabulary development (LEX A-Z) to articulate complex literary interpretations and thematic connections with precision.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course dedicates approximately three weeks to each of the ten selected Bildungsromans, tracing the coming-of-age journey across different cultural and historical contexts. Weekly sessions include LEX vocabulary quizzes, discussions on "Questions to Ponder" related to character growth and thematic purpose, and analysis of literary concepts such as symbolism, narrative reliability, and magical realism. Students will complete 5/5/5 essays for each novel, alongside shorter opinion/analysis responses and engagement with supplementary short stories (e.g., by Doris Lessing, Richard Wright, James Joyce, Kate Chopin) and critical articles (e.g., on Jane Eyre, Ralph Ellison, Joyce's writing style). A mid-term essay focuses on a deep thematic analysis of a chosen short story, and the course culminates in a final comparative essay on the "coming of age" nature of two novels studied.

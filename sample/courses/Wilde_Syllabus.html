Wilde: An Overview

Course Overview

This course offers an intensive overview of the diverse literary works of Oscar Wilde, a leading figure of the Aesthetic and Decadent movements in late Victorian England. Students will explore his celebrated novel, witty comedies, poignant fairy tales, critical essays, and confessional writings. The curriculum focuses on analyzing Wilde's dazzling style, his critiques of societal hypocrisy, his exploration of art, beauty, and morality, and the tragic dimensions of his life and career. Emphasis is placed on close reading, contextual understanding, and developing sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Aestheticism, Decadence, and Wit

Exploring Wilde's engagement with the Aesthetic movement ("art for art's sake"), Decadent themes, and his mastery of wit, epigram, and paradox as tools for social satire and philosophical commentary.

Themes of Art, Morality, and Performance

In-depth analysis of recurring themes in Wilde's work, including the relationship between art and life, appearance versus reality, societal masks and performance, moral responsibility, and the nature of beauty and sin.

Advanced Analytical Essays (5/5/5 Method) & Contextual Study

Crafting insightful 5/5/5 analytical essays on Wilde's literary techniques, thematic concerns, and cultural impact. Understanding his work within the context of late Victorian society and his own dramatic life. Continued mastery of LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around five-week intensives for major works like *The Picture of Dorian Gray* and *The Importance of Being Earnest*, with combined units for his shorter plays, fairy tales, essays, and later writings. Each unit involves close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and detailed discussions on Wilde's style, themes, and biographical context. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays, exploring the brilliance and complexity of Wilde's diverse literary output. The course aims to provide a comprehensive appreciation of one of literature's most flamboyant and thought-provoking figures.

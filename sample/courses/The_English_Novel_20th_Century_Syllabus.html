The English Novel: 20th Century

Course Overview

This course examines the dynamic evolution of the English novel throughout the 20th century, a period of profound artistic experimentation and societal change. Students will analyze works by key authors such as Conrad, Woolf, Joyce, Lawrence, Orwell, and Greene, exploring the impact of Modernism, the World Wars, existential concerns, and emerging postcolonial perspectives on narrative form and content. The curriculum emphasizes close reading of complex texts, understanding innovative literary techniques, contextual analysis, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) with LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Modernist and Postmodernist Techniques

Close reading to identify and analyze key characteristics of 20th-century narrative innovation, including stream of consciousness, unreliable narrators, fragmented narratives, dystopian visions, and intertextuality.

Thematic Exploration of 20th Century Concerns

Exploring how English novelists engaged with themes of alienation, the crisis of meaning, the impact of war, colonialism and its aftermath, totalitarianism, individual psychology, and changing social mores.

Advanced Critical Argumentation (5/5/5 Essays)

Crafting insightful analytical essays that interpret complex literary works, engage with critical perspectives, and articulate sophisticated arguments about style, theme, and cultural significance. Continued mastery of LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected major authors/works (or substantial excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar discussions on narrative strategies, thematic interpretations, and the socio-historical context of 20th-century Britain and its global connections. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, developing their ability to analyze and critique complex modern literature. The course aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the key developments in the English novel during this transformative century.

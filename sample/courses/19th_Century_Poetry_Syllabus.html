19th Century Poetry Syllabus

Course Overview

This course provides an in-depth survey of influential poetry from the 19th century, engaging with multiple poems through five-week intensives focused on core themes. Students will explore how poets captured individual experiences versus societal norms, depicted war, expressed diverse facets of love, confronted death and survival, narrated coming-of-age journeys, and responded to the dawn of modernity. The course emphasizes poetic analysis, understanding literary devices, and contextualizing works within their historical and cultural milieu, drawing from a wide range of English language poets of the era.

Educational Approach

Thematic Poetic Analysis

Students engage in deep thematic analysis of poems, writing 5/5/5 essay responses weekly. The course includes comparative analysis of poems and detailed study of how poets capture and convey complex ideas and emotions.

Introduction to Poetic Devices

Progressive introduction to and application of poetic devices and concepts across six distinct parts, enhancing students' analytical toolkit for interpreting poetry. Includes critical essays and literary analysis exercises.

LEX Vocabulary Enrichment

Comprehensive vocabulary development through the LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z), with regular quizzes to reinforce learning and ensure application in written analyses.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course is organized into six five-week thematic intensives: Individual Versus Society, War, Love, Death/Loss/Survival, Coming of Age/Growth, and Modernity. Each intensive explores 6-10 poems. Students will read 1-3 poems per week, complete 5/5/5 essay responses, and engage with critical literary analysis. LEX vocabulary is integrated throughout, with quizzes covering segments of the alphabet (A-D, E-H, etc.). The course culminates in a university-quality in-depth analysis unit essay on a poem of the student's choice.

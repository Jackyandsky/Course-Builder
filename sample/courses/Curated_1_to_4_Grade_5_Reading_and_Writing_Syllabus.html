Curated 1:4 Grade 5 Reading & Writing

Course Overview

This curated Grade 5 Reading & Writing course offers a stimulating exploration of diverse literary works, designed to enhance reading comprehension, critical thinking, and writing skills. In a supportive 1:4 teacher-student ratio, students will delve into classic and contemporary novels, focusing on themes of adventure, morality, social justice, and imagination. The curriculum emphasizes vocabulary development (LEX Intermediate A-Z), structured essay writing (3/5/5 and 5/5/5 methods), and active participation in literary discussions.

Educational Approach

Developing Analytical Writing

Introduction and practice with 3/5/5 and progressing to 5/5/5 essay structures, focusing on thesis development, providing textual evidence, and analyzing literary elements such as theme, character, and conflict.

Critical Reading and Discussion

Engaging with texts through "Questions to Ponder" to foster deeper understanding, critical interpretation, and the ability to articulate insights in class discussions and written responses.

LEX Intermediate Vocabulary

Systematic vocabulary building using the LEX Intermediate curriculum (Letters A-Z), with weekly quizzes and application in writing to expand students' expressive capabilities.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course is divided into units, each focusing on one of the eight selected novels for approximately 3-4 weeks. Weekly sessions in a 1:4 setting include LEX Intermediate vocabulary quizzes, discussions centered on reading comprehension and literary analysis, and progressive development of essay writing skills. Students will complete multiple written responses and essays for each novel, receiving personalized feedback. The course aims to build confidence and proficiency in reading and writing, preparing students for more advanced literary studies.

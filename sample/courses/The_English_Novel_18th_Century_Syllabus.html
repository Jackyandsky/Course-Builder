The English Novel: 18th Century

Course Overview

This course provides an in-depth exploration of the rise and development of the English novel in the 18th century, a period that saw this new literary form flourish. Students will analyze foundational works by key authors such as Defoe, Richardson, Fielding, Sterne, Smollett, and Burney, examining the novel's diverse forms (e.g., picaresque, epistolary, sentimental), its engagement with Enlightenment ideas, and its reflection of contemporary social and moral concerns. The curriculum emphasizes close reading, understanding narrative techniques, contextual analysis, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) with LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Early Novelistic Forms & Conventions

Close reading to identify and analyze the characteristics of emerging novel forms, including the picaresque, the epistolary novel, the novel of manners, and the sentimental novel. Examining narrative voice, plot structure, and characterization.

The Novel and 18th Century Society

Exploring how 18th-century novels engaged with contemporary social issues, moral debates, class structures, gender roles, and the influence of Enlightenment thought on individual and societal values.

Developing Critical Literary Arguments (5/5/5 Essays)

Crafting well-supported analytical essays that explore themes of virtue, social satire, individualism, and the evolving role of the novel as a vehicle for social commentary and moral instruction. Continued mastery of LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected pioneering authors/works (or substantial excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar discussions on narrative techniques, thematic concerns, and the historical and cultural context of 18th-century England. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, honing their ability to articulate complex interpretations of these foundational texts in English literature. The course aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the origins and early evolution of the English novel.

European Bildungsroman & Novels of Diaspora

Course Overview

This advanced course focuses on the Bildungsroman (coming-of-age novel) within the European literary tradition, with a special emphasis on narratives that also explore themes of diaspora and displacement. Students will analyze how protagonists navigate personal growth, identity formation, and societal integration, often while contending with issues of cultural alienation, exile, or migration. The course examines classic and contemporary European works, emphasizing critical analysis of character development, narrative strategies, and the intersection of personal journeys with broader socio-historical contexts. Rigorous essay writing (5/5/5 method) and LEX Advanced vocabulary are central to the curriculum.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Bildungsroman & Diaspora Themes

Exploration of genre conventions of the European Bildungsroman, combined with analysis of how themes like exile, cultural assimilation, memory, and the search for belonging are portrayed by authors dealing with diasporic experiences.

Character Development and Psychological Depth

In-depth study of protagonists' internal and external journeys, focusing on their psychological development, moral choices, relationships, and the influence of their cultural and geographical displacement on their coming-of-age.

5/5/5 Analytical Essays & LEX Advanced Vocabulary

Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays for each work, focusing on themes of identity, alienation, artistic growth, and societal integration. Comprehensive vocabulary development with LEX Advanced (Letters A-Z) supports nuanced interpretation.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around five-week intensives for each of the six selected novels. Each unit involves focused reading (approximately 100-125 pages per week), regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and detailed class discussions addressing "Questions to Ponder" specific to the Bildungsroman and diaspora themes. Students will complete multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, examining character arcs, narrative techniques, and the interplay of personal and cultural identity. The course includes critical essays on literary theory related to the Bildungsroman and diaspora studies, as well as contextual information on the authors and their respective European and diasporic settings.

Level 10-12 Advanced Reading: Science Fiction and Fantasy Syllabus

Course Overview

This advanced course for levels 10-12 explores the imaginative realms of Science Fiction and Fantasy literature. Students will trace the history of these genres from their roots in folklore and mythology to contemporary works, analyzing key tropes such as world-building, the hero's journey, societal critique through allegory, and the exploration of future technologies and alternative realities. The course aims to develop critical thinking, sophisticated literary analysis, and an understanding of how these genres reflect and question human nature and societal constructs. (Students will read approximately 100-150 pages per week and complete weekly writing assignments averaging 5-7 paragraphs.)

Educational Approach

Genre Conventions and World-Building Analysis

Examines the distinct elements of fantasy (myth, magic, epic quests) and science fiction (technology, future societies, alien encounters), with a focus on how authors construct believable and immersive worlds.

Thematic Exploration and Allegory

Students will analyze how Sci-Fi and Fantasy literature use allegory and speculative settings to explore complex themes such as good vs. evil, societal control, identity, environmentalism, and the human condition, drawing connections to real-world issues.

5/5/5 Essays & Vocabulary Development

Development of analytical writing through 5/5/5 essays focusing on character identity, antagonists, and the purpose of genre literature. Comprehensive vocabulary building using LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z) is integrated throughout.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session program is structured around ten core novels, typically dedicating three weeks to each. Weekly activities include LEX vocabulary quizzes, in-depth discussions on novel-specific "Questions to Ponder," and craft discussions on topics like the hero's journey, world-building, magical realism, irony, and futurism. Students engage with supplementary materials including critical articles (e.g., on Tolkien's influence, the purpose of fantasy, Philip K. Dick's prescience), short stories (e.g., Marquez's "A Very Old Man with Enormous Wings"), and educational videos (e.g., TED Talks on fantasy, Extra Sci-Fi analyses, Isaac Arthur on futurism). Writing assignments include 300-400 word responses, 5/5/5 essays, and a final essay focusing on Science Fiction and Fantasy literature as contemporary allegory.

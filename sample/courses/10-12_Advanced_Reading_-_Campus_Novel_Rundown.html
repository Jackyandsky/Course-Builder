Level 10-12 Advanced Reading: Campus Novel Rundown

Course Overview

This advanced course for levels 10-12 explores the theme of the campus novel, examining its relevance, the problems it seeks to solve, and its evolution in the modern era. Students will engage with various campus novels, analyze academic culture as depicted in literature, and develop their critical thinking and essay writing skills through discussions, article reviews, and structured writing assignments.

Educational Approach

Thematic Analysis of Academia

Critical examination of how campus novels lampoon, critique, and reflect academic life, education, and societal issues through literary analysis and discussion of related articles.

5/5/5 Essay Writing

Development of structured analytical writing using the 5/5/5 essay format, focusing on argumentation, evidence from texts, and adherence to format, with peer and teacher feedback.

Vocabulary Enrichment & Quizzes

Systematic vocabulary building using LEX curriculum (e.g., 20 words from LEX Letter A weekly) and words from texts, reinforced by regular quizzes and application.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course explores ten key campus novels, from Mary McCarthy's "The Groves of Academe" to Donna Tartt's "The Secret History". Each week includes reading assignments (approx. 70-100 pages), vocabulary quizzes, novel review and topic discussions (e.g., 'comedy of manners', necessity of the campus novel), and essay response assignments (opinion/argumentative or 5/5/5 essays). Students also engage with supplementary articles from sources like The New Yorker and University Affairs Magazine. The course culminates in a final essay tying together themes from two novels read.

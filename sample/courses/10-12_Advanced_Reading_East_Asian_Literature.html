Level 10-12 Advanced Reading: East Asian Literature

Course Overview

This advanced course for levels 10-12 offers an immersive journey into the diverse and rich literary traditions of East Asia, including Japan, China, Korea, and nations of Southeast Asia. Students will explore how storytelling has evolved from oral to written traditions, influenced by cultural, historical, and philosophical developments such as Buddhism. The course examines unique narrative styles, themes of identity, fate, magical realism, and societal change, contrasting them with Western literary conventions while fostering critical analysis and essay writing skills.

Educational Approach

Cultural & Historical Contextualization

Analysis of literature within the socio-historical contexts of East Asian countries, including discussions on mythology, the legacy of events like the Sino-Japanese War, the Cultural Revolution, the Vietnam War, and the complexities of diaspora identity.

Thematic & Stylistic Analysis (5/5/5 Essays)

Students will write analytical 5/5/5 essays focusing on themes such as magical realism (Murakami, Yan), historical allegory (Mo Yan, Thien, Nguyen), gender and societal roles (Kang, Cao Xueqin), and philosophical explorations (Mishima, Liu), paying attention to unique narrative structures and perspectives.

LEX Vocabulary & Critical Engagement

Systematic vocabulary development (LEX Letters A-Z) alongside critical reading of supplementary articles on topics like Chinese classical novels, the art of translation, and the impact of historical events on literature, to deepen analytical capabilities.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session program dedicates approximately 2-3 weeks to each of the ten selected works from East Asian literature. Each unit involves reading assignments (100-150 pages weekly), LEX vocabulary quizzes, and detailed discussions of "Questions to Ponder" focusing on character development (e.g., Kafka Tamura, the Monkey King), narrative techniques (e.g., use of vignettes, frame narratives), and thematic significance. Students will complete 5/5/5 essays, analytical responses to articles and short stories (e.g., Mishima's "Patriotism," Pearl S. Buck's "The Old Demon"), and engage with multimedia resources (e.g., videos on the Vietnam War, Chinese history). The course culminates in a final essay based on an independently chosen novel from the region or a comparative thematic analysis.

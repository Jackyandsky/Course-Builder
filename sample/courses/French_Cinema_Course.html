French Cinema Course

Course Overview

This course offers an immersive exploration of French cinema, tracing its rich history from early innovations to contemporary masterpieces. Students will analyze key movements such as Poetic Realism and the Nouvelle Vague (New Wave), study the works of influential directors, and examine how French films reflect and shape cultural, social, and political conversations. The curriculum emphasizes developing skills in film analysis, understanding cinematic language, and articulating interpretations through analytical writing and discussion, supported by LEX vocabulary tailored to film studies.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Cinematic Language & Movements

Students will learn to dissect films by analyzing narrative structure, mise-en-scène, cinematography (e.g., camera angles, movement), editing, sound design, and performance. Focus on understanding distinct French film movements like the New Wave and their impact.

Auteur Theory and Directorial Styles

Exploration of the concept of the "auteur" in French cinema, examining the recurring themes, stylistic signatures, and philosophical concerns of influential directors such as Renoir, Godard, Truffaut, Varda, and others.

Cultural Context & Critical Film Essays

Analyzing films within their French historical, social, and cultural contexts. Students will develop their critical writing skills through analytical essays and responses (e.g., 5/5/5 format), supported by LEX vocabulary related to film theory and criticism.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around the study of ten landmark French films, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each. Each unit includes film viewing assignments, in-depth class discussions on cinematic techniques and thematic interpretations, and analysis of critical readings about the films and their directors. LEX vocabulary quizzes (Letters A-Z) are integrated throughout. Students will write analytical essays focusing on aspects like genre conventions, directorial vision, or a film's socio-cultural significance. The course aims to provide a robust understanding of French cinema's artistic achievements and its global influence.

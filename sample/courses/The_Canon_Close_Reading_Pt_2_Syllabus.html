The Canon: Close Reading Part II - Medieval & Early Renaissance Voices

Course Overview

This second course in "The Canon: Close Reading" series delves into significant literary works from the Medieval and Early Renaissance periods. Students will engage in intensive textual analysis of influential authors and anonymous works that shaped Western literature, exploring themes of faith, chivalry, courtly love, societal structures, and the emerging humanist spirit. The curriculum emphasizes understanding period-specific language and conventions, developing advanced interpretive skills, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Close Reading of Period-Specific Texts

Developing skills to meticulously analyze Middle English, early vernacular Italian and French texts (in translation), focusing on allegory, symbolism, narrative voice, and poetic forms (e.g., terza rima, frame narrative).

Analysis of Medieval & Early Renaissance Themes

Understanding core themes such as the Great Chain of Being, courtly love, chivalric codes, religious allegory, social satire, and the emerging emphasis on individual experience and classical learning.

Developing Interpretive Arguments (5/5/5 Essays)

Crafting well-supported interpretive arguments about the texts, focusing on thematic complexity, character analysis within historical context, and the cultural significance of these foundational works. LEX Advanced Vocabulary (A-Z) is integral.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected authors/works (or significant excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth discussions on literary techniques, thematic interpretations, and the historical/cultural milieu of the Medieval and Early Renaissance periods. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, honing their ability to articulate complex interpretations with textual precision and historical awareness. The course aims to build a strong understanding of the literary developments leading into the High Renaissance.

Italian Cinema Course

Course Overview

This course offers a captivating journey through the rich and diverse landscape of Italian cinema. Students will explore key cinematic movements such as Neorealism, analyze the works of master directors like Fellini, Antonioni, and Leone, and examine how Italian films have reflected and influenced Italy's culture, history, and society. The curriculum emphasizes developing critical film analysis skills, understanding cinematic language specific to Italian traditions, and articulating interpretations through analytical writing and discussion, supported by LEX vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Italian Cinematic Styles & Movements

Students will analyze films focusing on narrative structure, visual aesthetics, cinematography, and sound. Emphasis on key movements like Italian Neorealism and popular genres such as Spaghetti Westerns and Giallo horror.

Auteur Study and Socio-Cultural Context

Exploration of influential Italian directors and their distinct thematic concerns and stylistic signatures. Films are analyzed within their specific Italian historical, social, and political contexts (e.g., post-WWII reconstruction, economic boom, Years of Lead).

Critical Film Essays & LEX Vocabulary

Development of critical writing skills through analytical essays (e.g., 5/5/5 format) on film themes, directorial techniques, and cultural impact. LEX vocabulary tailored to film studies and Italian cultural concepts is integrated.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around the study of ten landmark Italian films, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each. Units include film viewing assignments, intensive class discussions on cinematic elements and thematic interpretations, and analysis of critical readings related to Italian cinema. LEX vocabulary quizzes (Letters A-Z) are incorporated. Students will write analytical essays focusing on genre, directorial vision, or a film's reflection of Italian identity and history. The course aims to cultivate a profound appreciation for the artistry and influence of Italian cinema.

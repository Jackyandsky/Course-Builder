The Canon: Close Reading Part XV - Global Contemporary Voices

Course Overview

This fifteenth course in "The Canon: Close Reading" series explores a diverse range of influential works by contemporary global authors from the late 20th and early 21st centuries. Students will engage in intensive textual analysis of novels and short stories that reflect pressing global themes, innovative narrative styles, and diverse cultural perspectives. The course aims to further enhance advanced interpretive skills, engage with contemporary literary criticism, and cultivate sophisticated analytical writing through the 5/5/5 essay method and LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Diverse Global Narratives

Close reading of complex contemporary narratives from Japan, India, Germany/UK, Canada, UK, and Chile/Spain. Focus on stylistic innovations, magical realism, historical fiction, postcolonial themes, and explorations of memory and trauma.

Engagement with Contemporary Global Themes

Exploration of pressing global issues as reflected in literature, such as cultural identity in a globalized world, the legacy of historical injustices, the nature of storytelling, and the search for meaning in the contemporary era.

Advanced Critical Essays & Literary Theory (5/5/5 Method)

Crafting insightful and rigorously argued analytical essays that engage with the literary complexities and thematic concerns of the texts, potentially incorporating elements of contemporary literary theory. Continued emphasis on LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected contemporary global authors/works. Each unit involves intensive close reading of assigned texts or substantial excerpts, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and high-level seminar discussions exploring diverse literary techniques and critical interpretations. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, focusing on sophisticated literary analysis and critical engagement with contemporary global literature. The course aims to broaden students' literary horizons and refine their abilities to analyze and articulate responses to a wide spectrum of modern canonical works.

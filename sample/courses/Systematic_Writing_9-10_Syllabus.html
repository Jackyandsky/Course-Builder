Systematic Writing: Level 9-10

Course Overview

This transitional course for students in grades 9-10 is designed to refine systematic approaches to writing, bridging foundational skills with more advanced analytical, argumentative, and research-based composition. The curriculum focuses on developing complex arguments, understanding and applying rhetorical strategies, integrating research effectively, and enhancing stylistic sophistication. Students will engage with challenging model texts and develop a portfolio of well-structured and thoughtfully composed pieces, supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Key Writing Systems & Applications

Advanced Essay Structures & Organization

Moving beyond basic essay models to explore more complex organizational patterns (e.g., comparative, cause/effect, problem/solution). Developing sophisticated introductions, nuanced body paragraphs with integrated evidence, and impactful conclusions.

Developing Complex Arguments

Crafting precise and arguable thesis statements for complex topics. Developing multiple lines of reasoning with substantial supporting evidence. Effectively addressing and refuting counterarguments and alternative perspectives.

Introduction to Rhetorical Analysis

Identifying and analyzing basic and intermediate rhetorical strategies in literary and non-fiction texts (e.g., ethos, pathos, logos, irony, satire, figurative language). Understanding authorial purpose and audience impact.

Integrating Research and Source Material

Learning to effectively use research to support arguments in analytical and argumentative essays. Practicing summarizing, paraphrasing, quoting, and basic citation methods (e.g., MLA fundamentals).

Creative Writing with Purpose & Voice

Developing a distinct authorial voice in creative pieces (fiction, poetry, creative non-fiction). Focusing on thematic development, character complexity, and stylistic choices to achieve specific effects.

Advanced Grammar, Mechanics, and Style

Mastering advanced grammar concepts, punctuation (e.g., semicolons, colons, dashes), sentence variety (e.g., periodic and cumulative sentences), and stylistic choices for clarity, concision, and impact. LEX Advanced Vocabulary K-T.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Sophisticated Model Essays

Critical deconstruction of well-crafted essays from various disciplines to identify effective organizational strategies, argumentative techniques, rhetorical devices, and stylistic features.

Practice in Constructing Complex Arguments

Regular assignments focused on developing and supporting complex arguments on a range of topics, requiring students to engage with challenging ideas and synthesize information from multiple perspectives.

Workshops on Style, Voice, and Revision

Dedicated workshops to help students refine their writing style, develop an authentic authorial voice, and master advanced revision techniques for improving content, organization, clarity, and mechanics.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is designed to systematically enhance structural, argumentative, and stylistic writing skills essential for advanced high school and early college work. Units will focus on advanced essay forms, rhetorical analysis, research integration, and purposeful creative writing. LEX Advanced vocabulary (K-Z covered gradually) is a core component. Students will complete several major writing assignments, including analytical essays, research-supported arguments, and polished creative pieces, with an emphasis on systematic planning, drafting, and intensive revision. The course prepares students for the rigor of upper-level English courses and standardized tests requiring sophisticated writing.

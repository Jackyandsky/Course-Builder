Level 10-12 Advanced Reading: 20th Century American Literature, Female Writers

Course Overview

This advanced course for levels 10-12 delves into 20th Century American Literature by female writers, exploring their social, political, and economic situations. It focuses on analyzing themes, character development, and literary contributions within the context of women's history and the feminist movement in America. Students will engage with a diverse reading list, develop vocabulary, and hone their analytical writing skills through weekly assignments and unit essays.

Educational Approach

LEX Vocabulary & Contextual Words

Vocabulary is built from the LEX curriculum and unfamiliar words encountered in texts, reinforced by occasional quizzes and application in writing.

Analytical Essay Writing (5/5/5 Method)

Students will write, on average, 5-7 paragraphs per week, including 5/5/5 unit essays after each novel, focusing on themes such as the American female experience and literary representation.

Historical and Critical Contextualization

The course integrates women's history in America and critical articles from publications like The New Yorker and NPR to deepen understanding of the literary works.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session program explores an impactful sequence of novels by 20th Century American female writers, beginning with Willa Cather's "My Antonia" and concluding with Amy Tan's "The Joy Luck Club". Each week, students are expected to read approximately 100-150 pages. Sessions include vocabulary quizzes, in-depth discussions of novel content, analysis of "Questions to Ponder", and review of related articles. Writing assignments range from 300-400 word quick responses to comprehensive 5/5/5 essays following each novel. The course culminates in a final essay assessment based on an individually-chosen novel.

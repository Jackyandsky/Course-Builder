The Canon: Close Reading Part XII - Exploring Diverse Voices & Postcolonial Themes

Course Overview

This twelfth installment of "The Canon: Close Reading" series ventures into the powerful and diverse voices of postcolonial literature and authors exploring themes of cultural identity, the legacy of colonialism, and societal transformation. Students will engage in intensive textual analysis of influential works from Africa, the Caribbean, and the broader diaspora. The course aims to deepen understanding of global literary perspectives, refine advanced interpretive skills through engagement with complex narratives, and cultivate sophisticated analytical writing using the 5/5/5 essay method and LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Postcolonial Literary Analysis

Application of postcolonial critical theories to analyze themes of identity, hybridity, resistance, nationalism, and the impact of colonial legacies on individuals and societies as depicted in the texts.

Close Reading of Diverse Narrative Styles

Mastering techniques for analyzing varied narrative structures, oral storytelling traditions reflected in written texts, use of indigenous languages and creoles, and unique stylistic innovations by authors from different cultural backgrounds.

Sophisticated Thematic Essays (5/5/5 Method)

Crafting nuanced and well-supported analytical essays that engage with the complex socio-political, historical, and cultural dimensions of the selected works. Continued emphasis on precision through LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected authors/works. Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar-style discussions exploring literary questions and diverse critical viewpoints relevant to postcolonial studies. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, focusing on advanced literary analysis and critical engagement with global themes. The course aims to equip students with the skills to confidently interpret and articulate insights on a wide range of world literature.

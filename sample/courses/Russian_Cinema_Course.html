Russian Cinema Course

Course Overview

This course offers a dynamic exploration of Russian and Soviet cinema, from the groundbreaking experiments of the early Soviet era to contemporary Russian filmmaking. Students will analyze iconic films, study influential directors like Eisenstein and Tarkovsky, and examine key cinematic movements. The curriculum focuses on understanding how Russian cinema has reflected and responded to profound historical, political, and social transformations, while also developing critical film analysis skills and vocabulary related to film studies and Russian culture.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Russian Cinematic Styles & Movements

Focus on Soviet Montage theory (Eisenstein, Vertov), poetic cinema (Tarkovsky), Socialist Realism, and post-Soviet cinematic trends. Analysis of narrative structure, visual symbolism, cinematography, and sound design.

Auteur Study and Socio-Political Context

Exploration of influential Russian and Soviet directors and their unique artistic visions. Films are analyzed within their specific historical contexts, including the Russian Revolution, Stalinism, the Thaw, Perestroika, and contemporary Russia.

Critical Film Essays & LEX Vocabulary

Development of critical writing skills through analytical essays (e.g., 5/5/5 format) on film themes, directorial techniques, and cultural representation. LEX vocabulary tailored to film studies and Russian cultural and historical concepts will be integrated.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around the study of ten significant Russian and Soviet films, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each. Units include film viewing assignments, intensive class discussions on cinematic language and thematic content, and analysis of critical readings on Russian cinema. LEX vocabulary quizzes (Letters A-Z) are incorporated. Students will write analytical essays focusing on aspects such as genre, directorial intent, or a film's engagement with Russian history and society. The course aims to cultivate a deep understanding of Russian cinema's artistic legacy and its ongoing global relevance.

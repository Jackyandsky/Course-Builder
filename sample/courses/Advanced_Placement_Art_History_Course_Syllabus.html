Advanced Placement Art History

Course Overview

This Advanced Placement Art History course offers a comprehensive college-level survey of global art from prehistoric times to the contemporary era. Students will explore diverse artistic traditions across cultures, focusing on the 250 required works of art. The curriculum emphasizes developing skills in visual and contextual analysis, understanding form, function, content, and context, and making connections across artistic traditions. The course is designed to prepare students thoroughly for the AP Art History exam through rigorous study, analytical writing, and practice with exam formats.

Key Artistic Periods & Representative Works

Global Prehistory & Ancient Mediterranean

Lascaux Caves, Standard of Ur, Palette of King Narmer, Great Pyramids, Parthenon, Augustus of Primaporta, Colosseum.

Early Europe & Colonial Americas

Church of Sainte-Foy, Chartres Cathedral, Arena Chapel, Codex Mendoza, Frontispiece of the Codex Mendoza.

Later Europe & Americas (c. 1750-1980 C.E.)

Michelangelo's David, Vermeer's Woman Holding a Balance, Fragonard's The Swing, Goya's And There's Nothing to Be Done, Monet's The Saint-Lazare Station, Van Gogh's Starry Night, Picasso's Les Demoiselles d'Avignon, Warhol's Marilyn Diptych.

Indigenous Americas

Chavín de Huántar, Mesa Verde cliff dwellings, Great Serpent Mound, Templo Mayor (Main Aztec temple), City of Cusco including Qorikancha, Machu Picchu, Transformation mask.

Africa, Asia (West, Central, South, East, SE), The Pacific

Great Mosque of Djenné, Conical tower and circular wall of Great Zimbabwe, Wall plaque from Oba's palace, Ndop (portrait figure), Taj Mahal, Forbidden City (China), Ryoan-ji (Kyoto, Japan), Nan Madol.

Global Contemporary (1980 C.E. to Present)

Basquiat's Horn Players, Sherman's Untitled (#228), Bill Viola's The Crossing, Frank Gehry's Guggenheim Museum Bilbao, Christo and Jeanne-Claude's The Gates, Ai Weiwei's Kui Hua Zi (Sunflower Seeds).

Educational Approach

Visual and Contextual Analysis

Students learn to analyze artworks by examining their formal qualities (materials, techniques, visual elements) and understanding their historical, cultural, religious, and socio-political contexts. Emphasis is placed on form, function, content, and context for each work.

Comparative Analysis & Art Historical Argumentation

Development of skills in comparing and contrasting artworks across cultures and time periods, and constructing art historical arguments supported by visual and contextual evidence. Students practice writing attribution, contextual, and comparative essays.

AP Exam Preparation

Focused preparation for the AP Art History exam, including practice with multiple-choice questions, free-response questions (long and short essays), image identification, and thematic analysis. Students utilize textbooks like Gardner's Art Through the Ages and Stokstad's Art History.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into ten units covering distinct cultural and chronological areas, from Global Prehistory to Global Contemporary art. Each unit focuses on a selection of the 250 required artworks. Weekly activities include lectures, image-based discussions, analysis of primary and secondary source readings, and written assignments such as short essays, comparative analyses, and practice AP questions. Students are expected to maintain a sketchbook/journal for notes and visual analysis. The course aims to cover approximately 12-15 works per week to ensure comprehensive understanding of the AP curriculum.

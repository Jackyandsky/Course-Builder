Jane Austen: An Overview

Course Overview

This course offers an intensive study of the major novels of Jane Austen, one of England's most celebrated and enduring novelists. Students will delve into Austen's keen observations of Regency society, her masterful use of irony and free indirect discourse, and her exploration of themes such as love, marriage, social class, and female agency. The curriculum focuses on close reading, contextual understanding of the period, character analysis, and the development of sophisticated analytical essays using the 5/5/5 method and LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Regency Society and Manners

Exploration of the social customs, class structures, economic realities, and expectations for women prevalent in Austen's time, and how these are reflected and critiqued in her novels.

Austen's Narrative Techniques & Irony

Detailed study of Austen's distinctive narrative style, particularly her use of free indirect discourse, witty dialogue, and subtle irony to convey character psychology and social commentary.

5/5/5 Analytical Essays & LEX Advanced Vocabulary

Development of advanced analytical writing skills through 5/5/5 essays on themes such as courtship, social propriety, individual morality, and the role of women. Comprehensive vocabulary building with LEX Advanced (Letters A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around five-week intensives for each of Jane Austen's six major novels. Each unit involves focused reading (approximately 100-125 pages per week), regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth class discussions based on "Questions to Ponder" related to plot, character development, social satire, and narrative style. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per novel. The course includes primers on the Regency period, critical essays on Austen's work and influence, and discussions on various adaptations of her novels to provide a rich contextual understanding.

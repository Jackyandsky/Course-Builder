Marlowe: An Overview

Course Overview

This course offers an intensive study of Christopher Marlowe, a groundbreaking playwright of the Elizabethan era and a contemporary of Shakespeare. Students will explore Marlowe's major plays, examining his powerful "mighty line" (blank verse), his creation of ambitious and often transgressive protagonists, and his innovative contributions to English drama. The curriculum focuses on analyzing themes of power, ambition, knowledge, religion, and morality within the turbulent context of Renaissance England. Emphasis is placed on close reading of dramatic texts, understanding theatrical conventions, and developing sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Elizabethan Drama & Marlovian Hero

Exploration of the conventions of Elizabethan theatre, Marlowe's innovative use of blank verse, and the characteristics of the "Marlovian hero"—typically an overreacher undone by ambition or desire.

Thematic and Character Study

In-depth analysis of recurring themes such as the pursuit of power (Tamburlaine), forbidden knowledge (Faustus), Machiavellian politics (Jew of Malta), and personal desire versus political responsibility (Edward II). Focus on complex character motivations.

5/5/5 Analytical Essays & LEX Advanced Vocabulary

Development of advanced analytical writing skills through 5/5/5 essays on Marlowe's plays, focusing on dramatic structure, poetic language, thematic interpretation, and historical context. Comprehensive vocabulary building with LEX Advanced (Letters A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around intensive study of Marlowe's major plays, dedicating approximately 5-7 weeks to each key dramatic work, with additional sessions for his poetry. Each unit involves close reading of the play, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and detailed discussions on "Questions to Ponder" relating to dramatic action, character psychology, poetic imagery, and thematic implications. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per play. The course includes primers on Renaissance humanism, Elizabethan politics and religion, and the theatrical world of Marlowe and Shakespeare to provide rich contextual understanding.

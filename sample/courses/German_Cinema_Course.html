German Cinema Course

Course Overview

This course provides an engaging survey of German cinema, from the influential Expressionist movement of the silent era to contemporary filmmaking. Students will explore key films, renowned directors, and significant cinematic movements, analyzing their artistic innovations, cultural reflections, and historical significance within the German context. The curriculum focuses on developing skills in critical film analysis, understanding cinematic language unique to German cinema, and articulating interpretations through analytical writing and discussion, supported by LEX vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of German Cinematic Language & Movements

Students will analyze films focusing on narrative structure, visual style (e.g., Expressionist set design, New German Cinema aesthetics), cinematography, editing, and sound. Key movements like German Expressionism and New German Cinema will be explored in depth.

Auteur Study and Historical Context

Examination of influential German directors (e.g., Lang, Herzog, Fassbinder, Wenders) and their characteristic themes and styles. Films will be analyzed within their specific German historical, social, and political contexts (e.g., Weimar Republic, post-WWII division, reunification).

Critical Film Essays & LEX Vocabulary

Development of critical writing skills through analytical essays (e.g., 5/5/5 format) on film themes, directorial choices, and cultural significance. LEX vocabulary tailored to film studies and German cultural concepts will be integrated.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around the study of ten significant German films, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each. Units will include film viewing assignments, intensive class discussions on cinematic elements and thematic content, and analysis of critical readings on German cinema. LEX vocabulary quizzes (Letters A-Z) are incorporated. Students will write analytical essays focusing on genre, directorial intent, or the film's reflection of German history and society. The course aims to cultivate a strong understanding of German cinema's artistic contributions and its role in global film history.

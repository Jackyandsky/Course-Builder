Level 1-3 Reading & Writing Foundational Course

Course Overview

This foundational course for levels 1-3 introduces students to classroom expectations and syllabus goals, focusing on establishing reading and writing levels through basic responses and comprehension. Students will learn written response formats and build vocabulary through weekly lists and quizzes. The course aims to develop an understanding of basic literary concepts and foster creative and analytical writing skills.

Educational Approach

Vocabulary Development

Systematic vocabulary building through weekly quizzes (e.g., "20 words from Letter A") and feedback to enhance language proficiency.

Reading Comprehension and Analysis

Weekly reading reviews focus on understanding texts, new vocabulary, and core concepts, supported by in-class reading activities to assess pronunciation and fluency.

Progressive Writing Skills

Introduction to various writing styles including creative, descriptive, and analytical writing, with structured assignments and teacher feedback to ensure forward progress.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-week program guides students from reading diagnostics and basic written responses to more complex analytical writing. Weekly activities include vocabulary quizzes covering letters A-Z, reading assignments from selected books and short stories, creative writing prompts, and craft discussions. The course features a midterm quiz and culminates in a final creative writing and analytical response assignment.

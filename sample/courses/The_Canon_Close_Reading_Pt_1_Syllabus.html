The Canon: Close Reading Part I - Ancient Foundations

Course Overview

This course, the first in an intensive series, is dedicated to the close reading and critical analysis of foundational texts from the ancient world that form part of the Western literary canon. Students will engage deeply with epic poetry, drama, and mythology, developing advanced interpretive skills and an understanding of how these ancient narratives have shaped subsequent literature and thought. The curriculum emphasizes meticulous textual analysis, exploration of literary devices, understanding historical and cultural contexts, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Techniques of Intensive Close Reading

Developing skills to meticulously analyze textual details, including diction, syntax, imagery, symbolism, and narrative structure, to uncover layers of meaning and authorial intent.

Analysis of Archetypes, Myths, and Epic Conventions

Understanding recurring archetypal characters (the hero, the trickster), foundational myths, and the conventions of epic poetry (e.g., invocations, epic similes, divine intervention) and Greek tragedy.

Developing Interpretive Arguments (5/5/5 Essays)

Crafting well-supported interpretive arguments about the texts, focusing on thematic complexity, character psychology, and the cultural significance of these ancient works. LEX Advanced Vocabulary (A-Z) is integral.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected foundational works (or substantial excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading of assigned passages, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes (covering letters A-Z cumulatively), and in-depth discussions on literary techniques, thematic interpretations, and historical/cultural contexts (e.g., ancient Greece, Rome, Anglo-Saxon England). Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, honing their ability to articulate complex interpretations with textual precision. The course aims to build a strong foundation for advanced literary study by engaging with the roots of the Western canon.

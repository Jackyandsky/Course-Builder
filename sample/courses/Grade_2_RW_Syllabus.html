Grade 2 Reading & Writing Survey Course

Course Overview

This comprehensive Grade 2 Reading & Writing survey course builds upon early literacy foundations to further develop students' skills and enjoyment of language. Through a variety of engaging stories, including GVL originals and adapted classics, students will enhance their reading fluency, deepen their comprehension of more complex texts, and practice writing well-structured paragraphs and imaginative narratives. The course emphasizes interactive learning, creative expression, and the systematic development of vocabulary and grammatical understanding.

Educational Approach

Developing Reading Fluency & Comprehension

Continued phonics review and practice with decoding strategies. Focus on reading with expression and understanding, identifying main ideas, supporting details, character traits, and making simple inferences.

Paragraph & Narrative Writing

Instruction on writing well-organized paragraphs with topic sentences and supporting details. Introduction to narrative writing, including developing simple plots, characters, and settings.

Vocabulary Expansion & Grammar Skills

Systematic vocabulary building related to story themes and academic language. Reinforcement of Grade 2 grammar concepts, including parts of speech, sentence types, and correct punctuation.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 2 Reading & Writing Survey course integrates reading and writing instruction around a selection of ten diverse texts. Each week will feature vocabulary building, phonics/fluency practice, guided reading activities, and writing workshops. Students will learn to respond to literature through discussions and written assignments, such as character descriptions, story summaries, and creative writing prompts. The course aims to cultivate independent readers and writers who are confident in expressing their ideas clearly and creatively.

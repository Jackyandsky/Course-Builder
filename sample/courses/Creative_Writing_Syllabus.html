Creative Writing Workshop

Course Overview

This workshop-based course is designed to cultivate and develop students' skills in creative writing across a variety of genres, including fiction, poetry, playwriting/screenwriting, and creative non-fiction. The curriculum emphasizes understanding fundamental craft elements, finding and honing a unique authorial voice, and mastering the art of revision. Students will engage in regular writing exercises, analyze published creative works as models, participate in constructive peer critiques, and build a portfolio of their original work.

Genres & Craft Elements to Explore

Fundamentals of Creative Writing

Understanding the basics: show vs. tell, voice, tone, style, theme, and the creative process. Introduction to different genres.

Fiction Writing

Crafting compelling narratives: character development, plot structure (Freytag's Pyramid), setting, point of view, dialogue, pacing, and writing impactful scenes. Focus on short story writing.

Poetry Writing

Exploring poetic language: imagery, figurative language (metaphor, simile), rhythm and meter, sound devices (alliteration, assonance), and various poetic forms (e.g., sonnet, haiku, free verse).

Playwriting & Screenwriting

Introduction to dramatic structure, character arcs in visual storytelling, dialogue for the stage/screen, formatting scripts, and creating engaging scenes.

Creative Non-Fiction

Exploring the art of true storytelling: memoir, personal essay, narrative journalism. Focus on blending factual accuracy with literary techniques, developing a strong voice, and ethical considerations.

The Art of Revision

Understanding revision as a crucial part of the writing process. Techniques for self-editing, responding to feedback, and refining work for clarity, impact, and polish.

Educational Approach

Workshop-Based Learning & Peer Critique

Students will regularly share their original creative pieces in a supportive workshop environment, providing and receiving constructive feedback to improve their craft and develop critical reading skills.

Analysis of Mentor Texts

Close reading and analysis of published works of fiction, poetry, drama, and creative non-fiction to understand how established authors utilize craft elements effectively. Authors studied may include Vonnegut, Orwell, Didion, Plath, and others.

Portfolio Development & Writing Exercises

Consistent engagement in writing exercises designed to stimulate creativity and practice specific techniques. Students will compile a portfolio of their revised work throughout the course. Vocabulary specific to creative writing and literary analysis (LEX Advanced) is integrated.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is divided into five major units, each spanning approximately six weeks: Fundamentals of Creative Writing, Fiction, Poetry, Playwriting/Screenwriting, and Non-Fiction. Each unit involves targeted readings of mentor texts, focused writing exercises, craft lectures, and workshop sessions. Students will work on developing pieces in each genre, with an emphasis on revision based on peer and instructor feedback. LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes will be administered regularly. The course culminates in the submission of a final portfolio showcasing the student's best revised work from various genres.

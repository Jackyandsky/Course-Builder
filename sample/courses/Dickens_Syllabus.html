Dickens: An Overview

Course Overview

This course offers an intensive overview of the major works of Charles Dickens, one of the most influential novelists of the Victorian era. Students will explore his masterpieces, examining his powerful social commentary, unforgettable characters, intricate plots, and distinctive narrative style. The curriculum focuses on understanding Dickens's critique of Victorian society, his portrayal of poverty, class, and industrialization, and his enduring literary legacy. Emphasis is placed on close reading, thematic analysis, contextual understanding, and developing sophisticated analytical essays through the 5/5/5 method and LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Social Critique and Victorian Context

Analysis of Dickens's sharp critiques of social injustice, poverty, industrialization, the legal system, and educational institutions within the historical context of Victorian England.

Characterization and Narrative Technique

Exploration of Dickens's mastery of character creation (from grotesque villains to pitiable heroes), his use of satire and sentimentality, complex plotting, and the impact of serial publication on his narrative structures.

5/5/5 Analytical Essays & LEX Advanced Vocabulary

Development of advanced analytical writing skills through 5/5/5 essays on themes such as social mobility, morality, childhood, and justice. Comprehensive vocabulary building with LEX Advanced (Letters A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around five-week intensives for each of the six selected novels by Charles Dickens. Each unit involves focused reading (approximately 100-150 pages per week), regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth discussions on "Questions to Ponder" related to plot development, character arcs, social commentary, and literary style. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, exploring the multifaceted nature of Dickens's fiction. The course includes primers on Victorian England, the Industrial Revolution, and critical essays on Dickens's life and work to provide essential context.

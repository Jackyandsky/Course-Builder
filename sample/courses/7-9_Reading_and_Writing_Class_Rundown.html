Level 7-9 Reading & Writing Foundational Course

Course Overview

This foundational course for students in levels 7-9 is designed to significantly enhance critical reading abilities, analytical thinking, and sophisticated essay writing skills. Through engagement with a diverse range of classic and contemporary literature, including novels, plays, and short stories, students will delve into complex themes such as social justice, human nature, existentialism, and societal critique. The curriculum emphasizes the mastery of the 5/5/5 essay format, comprehensive vocabulary development (LEX A-Z), and in-depth craft discussions on literary devices and narrative structures.

Educational Approach

Mastery of 5/5/5 Essay Writing

Rigorous practice and refinement of the 5/5/5 essay method (5 paragraphs, 5 sentences per paragraph, 5 vocabulary words), focusing on thesis development, evidence-based argumentation, critical analysis, and stylistic clarity.

Advanced Literary Analysis & Thematic Discussion

In-depth exploration of literary themes (e.g., social justice, dystopia, friendship, existentialism, coming-of-age), narrative techniques (e.g., unreliable narrators, symbolism), and authorial intent through guided "Questions to Ponder" and class discussions.

Comprehensive Vocabulary Enrichment (LEX A-Z)

Systematic vocabulary building through the complete LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z), with weekly quizzes and integration into written assignments to enhance precision and sophistication in language use.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-week program takes students on an intensive journey through eleven diverse literary works. Each unit, typically spanning 2-3 weeks, involves detailed reading, LEX vocabulary quizzes, and craft discussions on topics like understanding Shakespeare, analyzing satire, narrative essay writing, and exploring existential themes. Students will regularly practice the 5/5/5 essay format with feedback, complete in-class essays (e.g., on *King Lear* or a chosen Ray Bradbury story), and work towards a final analytical essay. The course aims to build strong foundational skills for advanced literary study.

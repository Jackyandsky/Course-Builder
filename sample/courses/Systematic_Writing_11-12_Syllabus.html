Systematic Writing: Level 11-12

Course Overview

This advanced systematic writing course for students in grades 11-12 is designed to cultivate mastery in structuring and executing various forms of sophisticated composition. The curriculum emphasizes logical organization, rigorous argumentation, in-depth research and synthesis, nuanced rhetorical analysis, and polished creative expression. Students will engage with complex literary models, including *Catch-22* and *Lolita*, to deconstruct advanced narrative and stylistic systems, applying these insights to their own substantial writing projects. The course is geared towards preparing students for collegiate-level writing demands.

Key Writing Systems & Applications

Systematic Argumentation & Persuasion

Mastering advanced argumentative structures (e.g., extended Toulmin model, Rogerian argument). Analyzing and avoiding logical fallacies. Crafting compelling persuasive essays with sophisticated reasoning and evidence.

Systematic Research & Synthesis

Developing advanced research methodologies for extended projects. Critically evaluating and synthesizing complex sources. Crafting long-form research papers with proper citation (MLA/APA) and annotated bibliographies.

Systematic Rhetorical Analysis

Conducting in-depth rhetorical analysis of complex literary and non-fiction texts. Understanding and applying nuanced rhetorical theories and strategies (e.g., analyzing irony, satire, stylistic voice).

Systematic Creative Composition

Developing extended creative writing projects (e.g., short story cycles, novel outlining, poetry chapbooks). Focusing on advanced narrative techniques, character depth, consistent voice, and thematic development.

Literary Model: *Catch-22*

Joseph Heller. Deconstructing its satirical systems, non-linear narrative structure, and thematic exploration of war and bureaucracy as a model for complex critical and creative writing.

Literary Model: *Lolita*

Vladimir Nabokov. Analyzing its unreliable narration, intricate stylistic systems, complex character psychology, and controversial themes to understand authorial control and reader manipulation.

Educational Approach

Process-Oriented Writing Workshops

Intensive workshops focusing on the entire writing process for substantial pieces: ideation, outlining, drafting, multiple revisions, and polishing. Emphasis on peer editing for advanced structural and stylistic concerns.

Advanced Rhetorical Theory & Application

Study and application of advanced rhetorical theories to both analyze texts and improve students' own persuasive and analytical writing. Focus on audience awareness and purpose-driven communication.

Independent Research & Scholarly Inquiry

Guidance on conducting independent research projects, developing research questions, managing large amounts of information, and contributing to scholarly conversations through well-supported written arguments.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around major writing projects that allow for deep engagement with systematic approaches to composition. These may include an extended research paper, a creative writing portfolio, and an in-depth rhetorical analysis of a significant text or discourse. The study of literary models like *Catch-22* and *Lolita*, alongside challenging essays and articles, provides inspiration and analytical fodder. The course aims to equip students with the systematic thinking and writing skills necessary for success in higher education and beyond.

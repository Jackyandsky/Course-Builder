The English Novel: 19th Century

Course Overview

This course offers a comprehensive study of the English novel during the 19th century, a period marked by unprecedented literary output and the novel's rise to cultural prominence. Students will engage with major Victorian novelists such as Austen (as a bridge), Scott, Brontë, Dickens, Thackeray, and Eliot. The curriculum focuses on analyzing how these authors portrayed and critiqued their rapidly changing society, exploring themes of industrialization, social class, gender roles, morality, empire, and individualism. Emphasis is placed on close reading, understanding narrative techniques, contextual analysis, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) with LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Victorian Realism and Social Commentary

Close reading to identify the characteristics of Victorian realism, including detailed social observation, psychological depth in characterization, and overt or subtle social critique. Examining the novel as a tool for social reform.

Narrative Structure and Character Complexity

Exploring the intricate plot structures of Victorian novels (e.g., multi-plot narratives, serial publication's influence) and analyzing the development of complex, memorable characters who often embody societal types or challenge conventions.

Advanced Literary Argumentation (5/5/5 Essays)

Crafting insightful analytical essays that engage with themes of social mobility, gender expectations, industrial impact, moral development, and the individual's place in Victorian society. Continued mastery of LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the selected major authors/works (or substantial excerpts). Each unit involves rigorous close reading, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar discussions on narrative strategies, thematic interpretations, and the socio-historical context of 19th-century Britain. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, honing their ability to articulate complex literary arguments. The course aims to provide a profound understanding of the Victorian novel and its enduring significance.

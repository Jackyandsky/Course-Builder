Grade 1 Writing & Public Speaking

Course Overview

This introductory course for Grade 1 students focuses on nurturing fundamental writing skills and fostering early confidence in public speaking. Students will learn to form complete sentences, write simple narratives and descriptive pieces, and express their ideas creatively. The public speaking component aims to build comfort and clarity when sharing thoughts and stories with others in a supportive group setting. The course emphasizes interactive activities and positive reinforcement to make learning enjoyable and effective.

Key Skills & Activities Explored

Unit 1: All About Me & My World

Writing simple sentences about oneself, family, and favorite things. Drawing and labeling activities to connect images with words. Basic sentence structure (capitalization, punctuation).

Unit 2: Telling Simple Stories

Sequencing events to create short narratives (beginning, middle, end). Using picture prompts for storytelling. Introduction to descriptive words (adjectives).

Unit 3: Creative Expression

Writing simple poems and rhymes. Creating imaginative stories with characters and settings. Using drawing to illustrate written work.

Unit 4: Early Public Speaking

Participating in "Show and Tell" activities. Practicing clear articulation and listening skills. Reciting short, familiar poems or rhymes for the group.

Unit 5: Sharing Our Writing

Reading aloud one's own simple sentences and stories to peers. Offering positive comments on classmates' work. Building confidence in sharing ideas.

Vocabulary & Basic Grammar

Introducing age-appropriate vocabulary related to themes. Reinforcing correct use of pronouns, simple verb tenses, and plural nouns through writing activities.

Educational Approach

Guided Writing & Sentence Building

Step-by-step instruction on forming sentences, using writing frames, and organizing simple ideas into coherent written pieces. Focus on legible handwriting or keyboarding basics.

Interactive Storytelling & Speaking Games

Engaging students through interactive storytelling, role-playing, and speaking games designed to build vocabulary, fluency, and confidence in oral expression.

Positive Reinforcement & Confidence Building

Creating a supportive and encouraging environment where students feel comfortable taking risks in their writing and speaking. Emphasis on effort and participation.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 1 Writing & Public Speaking course is organized into thematic units that progressively build skills. Each unit, typically spanning 5-6 weeks, integrates writing and speaking activities. Lessons focus on developing specific writing conventions (e.g., sentence structure, basic punctuation) and public speaking techniques (e.g., speaking clearly, making eye contact). Students will participate in various writing tasks, from labeling and list-making to crafting short personal narratives and imaginative stories. Public speaking activities include sharing work, participating in discussions, and simple presentations. The course aims to lay a strong foundation for effective written and oral communication.

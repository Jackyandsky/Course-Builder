Grade 3 Reading

Course Overview

This Grade 3 Reading course is designed to transition students towards more independent reading and deeper comprehension of a variety of texts. Through engaging with a diverse selection of chapter books and stories, students will enhance their reading fluency, expand their vocabulary, and develop critical thinking skills. The curriculum focuses on analyzing character development, understanding plot structures, identifying themes, and making connections between literature and their own experiences, fostering a lasting appreciation for reading.

Educational Approach

Developing Deeper Comprehension

Focus on strategies such as summarizing main ideas, predicting outcomes, asking clarifying questions, visualizing story elements, and identifying cause and effect relationships within texts.

Character and Theme Analysis

Guiding students to analyze character traits, motivations, and changes throughout a story. Introduction to identifying and discussing underlying themes and author's purpose.

Vocabulary in Context & Fluency Practice

Expanding vocabulary through contextual clues in reading and direct instruction. Continued practice in reading with appropriate speed, accuracy, and expression to enhance fluency and comprehension.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 3 Reading course is structured around the exploration of ten diverse literary works, with approximately three weeks dedicated to each book or collection of stories. Each unit includes guided reading sessions, interactive discussions focusing on comprehension strategies and literary elements, and activities to build vocabulary and fluency. Students will learn to respond to literature by identifying key details, making connections, and expressing their interpretations. The course aims to build confident, engaged, and thoughtful readers prepared for the challenges of more complex texts.

Advanced Placement English Literature and Composition

Course Overview

This Advanced Placement English Literature and Composition course is a college-level program designed to engage students in the careful reading and critical analysis of imaginative literature. Through the close reading of selected texts from various genres and periods—including fiction, poetry, and drama—students will deepen their understanding of how writers use language to provide both meaning and pleasure. The course emphasizes developing skills in literary interpretation, analytical essay writing, and understanding literary elements, preparing students thoroughly for the AP English Literature and Composition exam.

Literary Explorations: Representative Works & Concepts

Prose Fiction Analysis

Studying novels and short stories (e.g., *Frankenstein*, *The Great Gatsby*, *Their Eyes Were Watching God*, short fiction by Poe, Hawthorne, Faulkner) to analyze character, setting, structure, narrative perspective, and theme.

Poetry Analysis

Interpreting poetry (e.g., works by Shakespeare, Donne, Keats, Dickinson, Frost, Eliot, Plath) by examining figurative language, imagery, symbolism, tone, rhythm, and form.

Drama Analysis

Analyzing plays (e.g., *Hamlet*, *A Doll's House*, *Death of a Salesman*) for elements such as plot, characterization, dialogue, stagecraft, and thematic development.

Literary Elements & Techniques

Identifying and analyzing the function of literary elements like plot, setting, character, point of view, theme, tone, irony, symbolism, and figurative language (metaphor, simile, personification, etc.).

Structure and Form

Understanding how the structure and form of literary works contribute to their meaning, including narrative structure in fiction, poetic forms (sonnet, villanelle, free verse), and dramatic structure.

Context and Interpretation

Considering historical, cultural, and biographical contexts that may inform literary interpretation, while focusing on evidence-based analysis of the text itself.

Educational Approach

Intensive Close Reading

Developing the ability to read complex literary texts with attention to detail, nuance, and ambiguity. Emphasis on annotation and identifying textual evidence to support interpretations.

Analytical Essay Writing

Regular practice in writing well-structured analytical essays that make claims about literature and support them with specific textual evidence and insightful commentary. Focus on poetry analysis essays, prose fiction analysis essays, and literary argument (open-ended) essays.

AP Exam Preparation

Targeted instruction and practice for all sections of the AP exam, including multiple-choice questions analyzing literary passages (both poetry and prose) and free-response essays. Strategies for timed writing and understanding scoring rubrics are key.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into units focusing on different literary genres (short fiction, poetry, the novel, drama) and thematic explorations. Each unit involves intensive reading, Socratic discussions, and the development of analytical writing skills. Students will study a range of canonical and contemporary literary works, practicing close reading strategies and engaging with literary criticism. Vocabulary relevant to literary analysis is explicitly taught and reinforced. The course culminates in comprehensive review and practice for the AP English Literature and Composition exam.

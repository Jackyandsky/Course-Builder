Systematic Writing: Level 8-9

Course Overview

This intermediate course for students in grades 8-9 focuses on developing systematic approaches to analytical, argumentative, and creative writing. The curriculum emphasizes clear essay structure, logical paragraph development, effective use of evidence, and consistent revision strategies. Students will learn to organize their thoughts methodically and express them with increasing clarity and precision, supported by LEX Intermediate and early Advanced vocabulary work.

Key Writing Systems & Applications

Systematic Essay Structure

Reinforcing the 5/5/5 essay model and introducing more flexible multi-paragraph structures. Mastering introductions with clear thesis statements, body paragraphs with strong topic sentences and evidence, and effective conclusions.

Systematic Paragraph Development

Focusing on unity, coherence, and logical flow within paragraphs. Practicing techniques for elaborating on ideas, integrating evidence smoothly, and using transitional words and phrases effectively.

Systematic Argumentation

Developing basic argumentative skills: forming clear claims, providing relevant reasons, supporting reasons with credible evidence, and beginning to address simple counterpoints or alternative views.

Systematic Creative Narrative

Planning and writing creative narratives with clear plot progression (exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution), developing consistent character voice, and maintaining a chosen point of view.

Systematic Revision Strategies

Learning and applying systematic checklists for self-editing and peer review, focusing on clarity, organization, support, grammar, and mechanics. Understanding revision as a multi-stage process.

Grammar & Style Enhancement

Targeted lessons on intermediate grammar, complex sentence structures, punctuation usage, and stylistic elements to improve written expression. Introduction to active vs. passive voice.

Educational Approach

Guided Practice in Outlining & Structuring

Step-by-step instruction and practice in creating effective outlines and structuring different types of essays and written pieces to ensure logical organization and coherence.

Analysis of Model Writing

Deconstructing well-written model essays and texts to identify effective structural components, argumentative techniques, and stylistic choices that students can emulate in their own writing.

Focused Peer Review Protocols

Utilizing structured peer review protocols that guide students to provide specific and constructive feedback on organization, clarity, support, and mechanics, fostering a collaborative learning environment.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is designed to systematically build writing skills across various genres relevant to grades 8-9. Units will focus on specific aspects of the writing process, from planning and drafting to revising and editing analytical, argumentative, and creative pieces. Regular writing assignments, paragraph-building exercises, and structural analyses of texts are core components. LEX Intermediate vocabulary (G-Z) and early LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-J) are integrated to enhance students' command of language. The course aims to instill a methodical approach to writing that students can apply across all academic subjects.

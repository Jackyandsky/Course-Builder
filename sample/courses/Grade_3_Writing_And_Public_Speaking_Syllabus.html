Grade 3 Writing & Public Speaking

Course Overview

This Grade 3 course focuses on developing more structured and expressive writing skills, alongside enhancing confidence and clarity in public speaking. Students will learn to craft multi-paragraph narratives, write simple informational reports, and begin to explore persuasive writing techniques. Public speaking activities will emphasize organization of ideas, vocal variety, and engaging an audience. The curriculum provides a supportive and interactive environment for students to grow as communicators.

Key Skills & Activities Explored

Unit 1: Developing Narrative Structures

Writing multi-paragraph stories with clear plot development (beginning, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution), engaging characters, and vivid settings. Focusing on descriptive details and dialogue.

Unit 2: Informational Report Writing

Gathering information from provided sources, organizing facts into logical paragraphs, and writing simple reports on topics such as animals, historical figures, or scientific concepts. Introduction to citing sources simply.

Unit 3: Introduction to Persuasive Writing

Learning to state an opinion clearly and support it with reasons and simple evidence. Understanding the concept of audience and purpose in persuasive texts. Writing simple opinion pieces or letters.

Unit 4: Exploring Poetry & Figurative Language

Experimenting with different poetic forms (e.g., haiku, limerick, free verse). Introduction to figurative language such as similes and metaphors to enhance creative expression.

Unit 5: Enhancing Public Speaking Delivery

Practicing organization of speeches with clear introductions, main points, and conclusions. Developing vocal variety (tone, pace, volume) and using simple visual aids. Participating in structured debates or presentations.

Grammar, Mechanics & Vocabulary

Reinforcing Grade 3 grammar (complex sentences, verb tenses, punctuation) and mechanics. Expanding vocabulary through thematic units and targeted word study relevant to writing and speaking tasks.

Educational Approach

Structured Writing Instruction

Providing explicit instruction on the writing process for different genres, including planning, drafting, revising for content and organization, and editing for grammar and mechanics.

Practical Public Speaking Exercises

Engaging students in a variety of practical speaking exercises, from impromptu talks to prepared presentations, to build skills in organization, delivery, and audience engagement.

Constructive Feedback and Goal Setting

Offering specific, constructive feedback on both written work and oral presentations. Helping students set personal goals for improvement as communicators.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 3 Writing & Public Speaking course is organized into units that focus on specific writing genres and complementary public speaking skills. Each unit, typically spanning 5-6 weeks, includes direct instruction, modeling, guided practice, independent work, and opportunities for sharing and presenting. Students will complete various writing projects, such as imaginative narratives, informational reports, persuasive paragraphs, and poems. Public speaking activities will include delivering short speeches, participating in debates, and oral storytelling. The course aims to equip students with the tools to express themselves effectively and confidently in both written and spoken forms.

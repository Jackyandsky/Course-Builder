The Canon: Close Reading Part VII - Romanticism & Revolution

Course Overview

This seventh course in "The Canon: Close Reading" series explores the passionate and transformative literature of the Romantic period (late 18th to mid-19th century). Students will engage in intensive textual analysis of influential works by key Romantic authors from Britain and Germany, examining themes of nature, individualism, emotion, the sublime, imagination, and the impact of revolutionary ideas. The curriculum emphasizes advanced close reading of poetry and prose, understanding the philosophical underpinnings of Romanticism, and crafting sophisticated analytical essays (5/5/5 method) supported by LEX Advanced vocabulary.

Educational Approach

Analysis of Romantic Ideals and Poetics

Close reading of texts to identify and analyze core Romantic themes such as the power of nature, the celebration of the individual and emotion, the role of imagination and genius, and critiques of industrialization and societal convention. Study of Romantic poetic forms and innovations.

The Gothic and the Sublime

Exploration of the Romantic fascination with the Gothic (e.g., *Frankenstein*), the sublime aspects of nature, and the darker elements of human psychology as expressed in the literature of the period.

Contextual Understanding & Advanced Essays (5/5/5 Method)

Understanding Romantic literature within its historical context of political revolutions (American, French), social upheaval, and changing philosophical landscapes. Crafting insightful analytical essays using the 5/5/5 method and LEX Advanced vocabulary (A-Z).

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is organized into five-week intensives for each of the six selected Romantic authors/works. Each unit involves rigorous close reading of assigned poetry and prose, regular LEX Advanced vocabulary quizzes, and in-depth seminar discussions on literary style, thematic interpretation, and the influence of Romantic thought. Students will write multiple 5/5/5 analytical essays per work, developing their ability to articulate nuanced arguments about this pivotal literary movement. The course aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of Romanticism and its enduring legacy.

Grades 3-5 Reading & Writing

Course Overview

This comprehensive Reading & Writing course for students in Grades 3-5 is designed to build and enhance literacy skills across a range of engaging texts and writing forms. Students will develop deeper reading comprehension strategies, learn to analyze literary elements, and improve their writing proficiency in narrative, informational, and opinion pieces. The curriculum focuses on fostering critical thinking, expanding vocabulary, and mastering structured writing techniques such as the 3/5/5 essay method, with differentiated instruction to meet the needs of learners within this age band.

Educational Approach

Differentiated Comprehension Strategies

Employing a range of comprehension strategies (e.g., making inferences, summarizing, identifying main idea and supporting details, understanding author's purpose) tailored to the varying reading levels within Grades 3-5.

Structured Writing Development (3/5/5 Method)

Systematic instruction in the writing process, including planning, drafting, and revising narrative, informational, and opinion pieces. Focus on paragraph development and the 3/5/5 essay structure appropriate for the grade levels.

Vocabulary Enrichment & Literary Elements

Targeted vocabulary building related to the texts and academic language. Introduction and analysis of literary elements such as character, setting, plot, theme, and figurative language.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grades 3-5 Reading & Writing course utilizes a selection from the listed diverse literary works, with each text typically explored over 2-4 weeks. Weekly sessions integrate reading comprehension activities, vocabulary exercises (LEX Intermediate A-Z), grammar instruction, and writing workshops. Students will practice responding to literature through discussions, short answers, and multi-paragraph essays. The course is designed to build a strong foundation in literacy, encouraging students to become more analytical readers and more proficient, organized writers.

Level 8-9 Fundamentals of Writing

Course Overview

This intermediate writing course for students in grades 8-9 is designed to strengthen analytical and argumentative writing abilities, refine essay structure, and enhance creative expression. The curriculum focuses on developing more nuanced arguments, mastering complex sentence structures, engaging in thorough revision, and applying rhetorical principles. Students will analyze varied model texts and practice writing in multiple genres, supported by LEX Intermediate and Advanced vocabulary building.

Key Writing Units & Concepts

Unit 1: Review & Reinforce Fundamentals

Consolidating understanding of thesis statements, claims, evidence, argumentation, and effective essay structure (e.g., 5/5/5 method). Addressing common grammatical errors.

Unit 2: Intermediate Grammar & Stylistics

Mastering complex sentence structures (e.g., compound-complex), advanced punctuation (colons, semicolons), varying sentence beginnings, and developing a more sophisticated writing style. LEX Intermediate Vocabulary G-L.

Unit 3: The In-Depth Editorial Process

Developing advanced self-editing skills, providing constructive peer feedback with precision, and implementing revision strategies for clarity, coherence, and impact. LEX Intermediate Vocabulary M-R.

Unit 4: Analytical & Argumentative Essays

Crafting well-supported analytical essays on literary and non-fiction texts. Developing nuanced arguments, addressing counterarguments effectively, and employing basic rhetorical devices. LEX Intermediate Vocabulary S-Z.

Unit 5: Creative Writing Exploration

Exploring voice and narrative techniques in short fiction and creative non-fiction. Experimenting with figurative language, dialogue, and character development. Introduction to LEX Advanced Vocabulary A-E.

Unit 6: Introduction to Research Skills

Learning basic research methodologies, how to evaluate sources for credibility, summarizing and paraphrasing information, and understanding the importance of avoiding plagiarism. Introduction to basic citation. LEX Advanced Vocabulary F-J.

Educational Approach

Workshop-Style Learning and Peer Review

Engaging in writing workshops that emphasize constructive peer review and collaborative learning. Students refine their work based on feedback and learn to critique effectively.

Analysis of Diverse Model Texts

Close reading and analysis of a variety of model texts, including essays, articles, short stories, and excerpts from longer works, to understand effective writing strategies and stylistic choices.

Portfolio Development and Revision Focus

Emphasis on the writing process, with a significant focus on revision. Students will develop a portfolio of their best work, showcasing growth in different writing styles and techniques.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session course is structured around six key writing units, each spanning approximately five weeks. Units cover: Review of Fundamentals; Intermediate Grammar & Stylistics; The In-Depth Editorial Process; Analytical & Argumentative Essays; Creative Writing Exploration; and an Introduction to Research Skills. Each unit involves direct instruction, analysis of model texts, writing exercises, and workshop sessions. LEX Intermediate and the initial stages of LEX Advanced vocabulary are integrated weekly. The course aims to equip students with the skills to write with greater clarity, complexity, and confidence across various academic and creative contexts.

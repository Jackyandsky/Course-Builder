20th Century Poetry

Course Overview

This course offers a comprehensive survey of influential poetry from the 20th century, engaging with a diverse range of voices and styles. Students will explore how poets responded to themes of modernity, alienation, societal upheaval, war, love, loss, and personal identity through thematic intensives. The curriculum emphasizes the development of analytical skills for interpreting poetic language, form, and devices, alongside contextual understanding and vocabulary enrichment through the LEX program.

Educational Approach

Thematic and Formal Poetic Analysis

Students engage in deep analysis of poems, focusing on themes, imagery, tone, and poetic forms (e.g., sonnet, free verse, confessional poetry). Weekly 5/5/5 essay responses and comparative analyses enhance interpretative skills.

Understanding Poetic Movements

Exploration of major 20th-century poetic movements such as Modernism, Imagism, the Harlem Renaissance, Confessional poetry, and Postmodernism, analyzing how these movements shaped poetic expression.

LEX Vocabulary Enrichment

Comprehensive vocabulary development through the LEX curriculum (Letters A-Z), with regular quizzes to reinforce learning and ensure application in written analyses of complex poetry.

Curriculum Structure

This 30-session course is organized into six five-week thematic intensives: Individual Versus Society, War, Love, Death/Loss/Survival, Coming of Age/Growth, and Modernity/Postmodernity. Each intensive explores a selection of representative poems. Students will read and analyze 1-3 poems per week, complete 5/5/5 essay responses, and engage with critical literary analysis. LEX vocabulary is integrated throughout, with quizzes covering segments of the alphabet (A-D, E-H, etc.). The course culminates in a university-quality in-depth analysis unit essay on a 20th-century poem of the student's choice.

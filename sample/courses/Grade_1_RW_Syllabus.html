Grade 1 Reading & Writing Survey Course

Course Overview

This foundational Reading & Writing survey course for Grade 1 students is designed to ignite a lifelong love of stories and language. Through a delightful collection of age-appropriate books and GVL original texts, students will be introduced to early literacy skills, including phonics, sight word recognition, basic reading comprehension, and emergent writing. The course focuses on creating a fun, interactive, and supportive environment where young learners can build confidence and essential skills for their academic journey.

Educational Approach

Phonics & Sight Word Foundations

Systematic introduction to letter sounds, phonics patterns, and essential sight words to build decoding skills and reading fluency. Engaging activities and games make learning fun.

Interactive Read-Alouds & Comprehension

Daily read-alouds of engaging stories to model fluent reading and develop listening comprehension. Activities focus on identifying main characters, settings, and key events.

Emergent Writing & Creative Expression

Encouraging early writing through drawing, labeling, dictating sentences, and attempting to write simple sentences and stories. Focus on expressing ideas and building confidence.

Curriculum Structure

The 30-session Grade 1 Reading & Writing Survey course is structured around weekly themes and a selection of ten engaging books/stories. Each week will introduce new vocabulary words, focus on specific letter sounds or phonics concepts, and involve interactive reading and writing activities. Students will participate in guided reading sessions, practice sight words, and engage in activities that connect reading to writing, such as drawing responses to stories or writing simple sentences about characters. The course aims to build a strong and joyful foundation in literacy.
